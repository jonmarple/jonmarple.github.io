1D Pong

TSU CS484: Embedded Systems - Final
The majority of my embedded systems course was focused on Arduino Uno development. We wrote nearly everything in assembly, although C was allowed on the larger projects. For our final project, my partner and I implemented a 1D version of Pong. This consisted of 2 buttons for paddle controls, 10 LEDs to display the ball, a buzzer to indicate hits and misses, and a potentiometer to change the speed of the ball!

Intron/Exon Alignment

TSU CS325: Bioninformatics - Final
The bioinformatics course I took was a co-major program consisting of computer science and biology students. For my team's final project, we developed a python program that would attempt to align introns and exons using dynamic programming. It would accept numerous large input files, process them for nearly 30 minutes, and return detailed output regarding the scores of the alignments.

Grandma's Pi

Raspberry Pi TTS Calendar Scraper
Context: My mother maintains a Google calendar for her mother's appointments.
Problem: My grandmother is constantly calling my mother to ask when her appointments are.
Solution: Rigging an original Raspberry Pi with a numpad and speakers to scrape a Google calendar, convert appointment information using Google's TTS, and playing that information back with OMXPlayer.

This allowed my grandmother to manually receive appointment reminders on her own!
(until the Raspberry Pi died...)

During my Linux phase in college, I spent a lot of time investigating automation ideas. I set up an Ubuntu Server instance to serve up media files for my Raspberry Pi Kodi client. PokeWeight

Reddit Bot
One of my first programming projects (judge lightly) was a Reddit bot that would detect weights in comments and determine a combination of Pokemon that would be equivalent to that weight using a sort of binary search, within 5% accuracy. It would respond to the post with the list of Pokemon!
